Needs vs. Wants.
It's Not That Simple.

Everyone thinks they know the difference. Most people are wrong.

You've heard it before: "Only buy what you need."

Cool advice. Except... what counts as a need?

Needs and wants aren't two neat boxes. They're a sliding scale that shifts based on your situation, your money, and what's happening in your life right now.
  • Food? Need. Eating out with friends? Less clear.
  • Clothes? Need. Those specific brand name shoes? Hmm.
  • A phone? Need. The newest model? That's a different conversation.

(Gets messy fast.)

Real talk
  • This isn't about labeling everything perfectly
  • It's about being honest with yourself about why you're spending
  • "I need this" and "I want this and I'm choosing it" are both fine
  • The problem starts when you call a want a need to justify it

Some are obvious. Some will start arguments.

Let's look at both types.

Context changes everything. The same item can be a need for one person and a want for another. That's reality.

Notice how many landed in "It Depends"? That is great because it shows you are questioning if it is a good use of money.

The skill is asking yourself one question every time: "Is this a need right now, or am I calling it a need because I want it?"
  • A haircut before a job interview IS different from a haircut because you're bored
  • New shoes when yours are falling apart IS different from new shoes because a fresh colorway dropped
  • Same item. Different situation. Different answer.
The trick your brain plays
  • Your brain is really good at upgrading wants to needs
  • "I NEED those shoes." No you don't. You want them.
  • And that's fine! Just call it what it is
  • The moment you're honest about it, you stop being surprised when your money disappears

⏰ The 24-Hour Rule

Before buying anything over $20, wait a day.

If you still want it tomorrow, go for it. You'd be shocked how many "needs" disappear overnight.
